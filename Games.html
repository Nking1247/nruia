Sticky Hands

  Sticky Hands is a VR Game made for the Oculus Quest. You play as Jeff. Jeff is very bored at his office today and needs to find something to do. Luckily, he was at the arcade yesterday and won a sticky hand toy! Control Jeff and cause as much chaos as you can. This is the first VR game that I have ever made which meant that I faced a lot of challenges. It took me 2 days just to get VR controls to work in our game. In addition, the sticky hand was not easy to make as we wanted it to feel realistic but also easy to use. By taking full advantage of the joint system in Unity, I was able to create a sticky hand which emulated the stickiness and elasticity of a real life sticky hand. However, this was very complicated to aim and therefore did not make an easy to play game. Therefore, I worked for days in order to create what I call fake physics. The first iterations tried to guess where the player wanted the sticky hand to be by taking in variables such as where the player was looking, how fast the player's hand was moving, and possible objects in the sticky hand's path. While this worked, we found it to be more intuitive to use a button which would shoot out the sticky hand in the direction of the player's hand. I am very proud of this game and cannot believe what I was able to accomplish in 1 week.


This game was made in one week with a team at Carnegie Mellon University. I was the lead programmer.

Hell Delver

  We were tasked with recreating Tempest, a thrilling 3D arcade game from 1981. After doing some research, we found that the creator of Tempest, Dave Theurer, was inspired by a nightmare he had of monsters crawling up a hole. We decided to bring his vision to life in our recreation. The game was revolutionary for being one of the first faux 3D games. We decided to keep this style of art by using 2D sprites with a 3D perspective to create a unique looking style that stays true to the original. In addition, Tempest was the first to have a level select system. For this reason, we created multiple levels with smooth transitions and my code is completely modular so it is easy to create new levels. Finally, Tempest was the first to use Vector graphics. For this reason, we decided to up the graphics in our recreation. I did this by learning post-processing and adding it to the game.


This game was made in one week with a team at Carnegie Mellon University. I was the sole programmer.
