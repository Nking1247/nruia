What I Learned

  sto·ry
 /stôrē/
  noun
  1.
  an account of imaginary or real people and events told for entertainment.

  Some games are definetely a form of story telling. For example, in Elder Scrolls: Skyrim, the player goes through a story to finish the game. However, games are different from conventional forms of story telling because they use choice. By this, I mean they audience can choose how the story develops. In AAA games with a lot of functionality like Mass Effect 2, choice is used to its full potential. The player can decide where the main character goes and what he/she does. However, there are games with less production value who can also give their players control over the story. In addition to dialogue and cutscenes, games can show a story through the game play itself. In BOTW, for example, the player gets stronger and fights larger enemies over time, showing the progress the character has made. Overall, games are a great way to present a story that allows some creativity to be put in the audience's hands.
